Selecting **Option B: Low-Code / No-Code Business App** means you will build a functional **Event Registration System** using visual development platforms rather than writing all the code from scratch.

### **Detailed Project Plan for Option B**

Your group should follow these steps to meet the project requirements:

1.  **Select a Platform:** You must choose a platform shown in Week 10, such as **Bubble**, **Anvil**, **Google AppSheet**, or **Microsoft Power Apps**. For example, **Anvil** is specifically recommended if your group wants to use Python for backend logic while using drag-and-drop tools for the interface.
2.  **Define the Business Problem:** Choose a specific type of event, such as a conference, workshop, or seminar, and identify the business need for an automated registration system.
3.  **Build the User Interface:** Your app must include:
    *   An **event information page**.
    *   A **registration form** for attendees.
    *   A **list of participants** for organizers.
    *   A **detail page** for each individual registration.
4.  **Set Up the Database:** You must include a database with **at least two related tables**, such as "Event" and "Registration".
5.  **Implement Management Logic:** The system must allow organizers to **edit or cancel** existing registrations.
6.  **Add Interactivity:** Even in a low-code environment, you must include at least one simple **JavaScript interaction** and basic styling.
